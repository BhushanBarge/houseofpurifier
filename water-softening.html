Our Service

water softening Service

Step 1: Softening the Water

"Water softening begins by passing hard water through the resin beads inside the softener. These beads are charged with sodium ions, which attract and replace the calcium and magnesium ions in the water that cause hardness. As the water flows through the resin, the hard minerals are removed, and the softened water is sent out for use. This process significantly reduces the scale buildup in pipes and appliances."

Step 2: Regeneration Process

"After the resin beads have absorbed too much calcium and magnesium, they need to be cleaned through a regeneration cycle. During regeneration, a brine solution (saltwater) is passed through the beads. This solution displaces the accumulated minerals, flushing them away. The beads are then recharged with sodium ions, ready to continue softening water. This process usually occurs automatically, often during periods of low water use, such as at night."
